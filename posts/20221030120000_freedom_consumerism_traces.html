Freedom, Consumerism, and the Price of Traces

30 October 2022, Helsinki, Åndrei Makarov

I keep returning to a mechanical image when people call freedom abstract: with physical objects we speak of degrees of freedom, ranges of movement, points where intention can still steer, and more freedom in that sense asks for more learning, more skill, more effort to keep control, so the word stops floating and starts behaving like an attribute measured inside conditions rather than a slogan hung above them.

Consumerism does not need instant feedback to function, yet the internet taught a particular hunger anyway, silent access to public information without announcing yourself, learning without leaving a request trail when the page was already open on the sidewalk, and that quiet reading felt like autonomy until the same architecture learned to leak, to profile, to sell the footprint you thought you were avoiding, so the benefit and the wound share one doorway.

The same tension shows up between people: connection wants transparency, boundaries want cover, professional life wants demonstration, private life wants room to fail without inventory, and I have felt the stress of standing inside someone else's vulnerability knowing my words will leave marks I cannot fully recall later, which is perhaps the honest price of work that is not merely transactional.

Career was never a clean line for me; coding began as community labor long before it became income, hundreds of small contributions whose reward was knowledge or recognition or simply the object existing, and I still wonder whether the marketplace lesson—demo, sell, buy, negotiate visibility—belongs to the same craft or sits beside it like a second language learned too late, uncomfortable precisely because the work itself was once enough.

When freedom is discussed as points of movement, control means steering the object according to intention inside those points, and I see the parallel in professional life as accountability inside expectations, transparency inside commitments, autonomy inside teams that still have to ship, always with emotions present, never optional, adding entropy to every decision we pretend was only technical.

Modern consumer infrastructure wants traces: accounts, cookies, cards, ledgers, confirmations, GDPR checkboxes that still record the click, blockchain proofs if you go that direction, security through visibility, participation through exposure, and I do not know how to participate in shared public life without paying some version of that price, only which traces I am willing to carry today.

I still make choices about what to expose, what to withhold, what freedom I am exercising in a given week, and the choices remain unfinished; the tension does not resolve into a policy, only into lived navigation where freedom shows up as capacity to change inside constraints, and where the traces I leave become part of the cost I am still learning to read.
